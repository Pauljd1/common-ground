About Common Ground

Unlocking Hemp's Potential for Soil and Food System Regeneration through rigorous scientific research and community collaboration.

The Scientific Gap

Soil degradation, contamination, and declining nutrient density in food represent a critical, interlinked challenge for global agriculture. Persistent pollutants such as PFAS have been detected in vegetables, eggs, and even human breast milk, posing risks to both ecosystem and human health. Current remediation methods are costly, disruptive, and insufficient.

Hemp (Cannabis sativa L.) has shown significant promise as a multipurpose crop that can remove contaminants like PFAS from soil, improve soil carbon content and biodiversity, increase yields of subsequent crops, support pollinator diversity, and provide sustainable fiber, oil, and food products. However, key knowledge gaps remain around PFAS remediation capacity and mechanisms.

Our Mission

To build the scientific foundation for hemp-based soil regeneration through rigorous research, creating an evidence-based framework for using hemp as a tool to restore soil health and improve agricultural outcomes while establishing an open-source seed bank for future research.

Our Values

Scientific Rigor

We conduct controlled trials and systematic research to build evidence-based solutions for soil remediation.

Open Access

Our research, methodologies, and germplasm remain accessible to ensure widespread benefit and continued innovation.

Environmental Restoration

We prioritize practices that actively restore contaminated soils and enhance ecosystem health.

Agricultural Innovation

We bridge environmental remediation with agricultural innovation to create sustainable land management strategies.

PFAS Remediation

Researching hemp's capacity to remove persistent pollutants from contaminated soils.

Soil Health Research

Studying how hemp improves soil carbon, water retention, and microbial activity.

Research Approach

Variety Screening

Cultivation of 20 hemp varieties under controlled and field conditions with comprehensive PFAS analysis.

Mechanism Study

Laboratory analysis to understand PFAS uptake pathways and storage compartments within hemp plants.

Seed Bank Development

Collection, conservation, and open-source licensing of high-performing hemp germplasm for future research.

Connecting people and land through regenerative action.
