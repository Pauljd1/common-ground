Soil degradation, contamination, and declining nutrient density in food are critical, interlinked challenges for global agriculture. Our project is building the scientific foundation for using hemp (Cannabis sativa L.) as a tool to remediate PFAS-contaminated soils, restore soil health, and improve agricultural outcomes.

Our Vision Research Approach

Why Hemp? The Scientific Opportunity

PFAS Remediation

Hemp can remove persistent pollutants like PFAS from soil via phytoremediation, offering a scalable, plant-based solution to contamination.

Soil & Ecosystem Benefits

Research shows hemp improves soil carbon, water retention, and biodiversity, and increases yields of subsequent crops in rotation.

Open-Source Genetics

We are building an open-source seed bank to ensure high-performing hemp varieties for remediation and regeneration remain accessible for research and agriculture.

Vision & Objectives

Building the Scientific Foundation for Hemp-Based Soil Regeneration

We envision a robust, evidence-based framework for using hemp to restore soil health and improve agricultural outcomes. Our research will identify hemp varieties with the highest capacity for PFAS remediation and soil regeneration, while establishing an open-source seed bank for future research and agricultural use.

  • Screen diverse hemp varieties for PFAS uptake and storage in roots, stems, fibers, and seeds.
  • Assess co-benefits for soil quality, biodiversity, and nutrient density of subsequent crops.
  • Develop standardised cultivation and rotation protocols for integrating hemp into regenerative agriculture.
  • Build an open-source germplasm repository to protect and share high-performing varieties.

Research Approach

Variety Screening

Cultivation of 20 hemp varieties (10 fiber, 10 oil/seed) under controlled and field conditions, with pre- and post-harvest soil sampling for PFAS analysis.

Mechanism Study

Laboratory analysis to understand PFAS uptake pathways and storage compartments within the plant.

Soil & Crop Impact

Monitoring of soil carbon, water retention, microbial activity, and biodiversity indicators; follow-on crop trials for yield and nutrient density.

Seed Bank Development

Collection, conservation, and licensing of germplasm under open-source agreements to ensure accessibility.

Open Data & Protocols

Open-access publication of results, methodologies, and recommended cultivation practices for remediation-focused hemp use.
